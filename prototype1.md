## Outline
The idea is that the player has to manage their ducks in order to destroy the geese tower and win the game
Proper management helps the player gain points/xp/money which they can use for upgrades
If the player destroys the enemy tower before their own tower is destroyed, they win

## Management
In this prototype I will probably turn the whole management part into a timing based clicker/puzzle game.
Basically the idea is that the duck tower has couple floors, each with its own "Module"
Each module has its own interaction to function properly, for example money printer which requires you to click buttons in correct order
Or more interesting idea: **a shield generator the player needs to keep on to protect their tower**
I also think it would be a good idea not to let the player (also probably the enemies) to regenerate health for their towers since that would turn it into a waiting game (Like the player only focusing on shields for a longer amount of time to regenerate some HP before they continue attacking.)
I think it would be an interesting idea to make attacking the enemy tower harder than just pressing one button. Loading the weapon itself could be a whole process

Getting more money, instead of upgrades, could be used to buy more rooms in the tower, giving more power to the player but requiring more interactions from the player, scaling the difficulty

## Progression
Since we have a clear end goal (destroying the enemy tower) instead of a endless loop, it would be crucial to add some form of progression.

**Two ideas that come to mind are:**
1. After you destroy the enemy tower, you move on to the next, harder tower, until you can no longer keep going, this approach would make the game more replayable, and would also fit the "reward" of the jam, the game being playable on the console thing, the only way to make a game made in such short time be fun for more than 10 minutes is to make it procedurally endless. There are some issues with this approach though, the space in the tower is limited, since it doesn't move on the screen, the screen itself is the limit for the amount of rooms we can fit in the tower. so there would be a point very early in the game where the player can no longer progress, so all that's left is interacting with the rooms the player already has, significantly removing part of the fun. this could be countered by allowing the player to merge simmilar rooms to achieve a better room, but it also has no way of being endless and would get boring rather quickly.

2. The game would have levels, where each is harder than the previous one. this might cause some time-related problems, but would fix any issue that any endless mode introduces, obviously that would limit the replayability of the game though, which might harm the chances of the game during rating/introducing to the console
